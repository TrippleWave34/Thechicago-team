2. Humble Beginnings

Before the dynasty, the Bulls were just another team trying to stay relevant. Michael Jordan was drafted in 1984, and it didnt take long for him to become a superstar.

Still, the Bulls weren't winning titles. The Pistons bullied them in the late '80s, and critics said MJ couuld't win without help. Enter Scottie Pippen.

Drafted in 1987, he wasn't a ready-made star, but he grew into the perfect counterpart. With Pippen's defensive prowess and unselfish playstyle, the foundation of something special was set in motion.

3. The Rise of a Dynasty

From 1991 to 1993, the Bulls went on a tear, winning three straight championships. MJ was electric, Pippen was quietly dominant, and role players like Horace Grant and John Paxson did their jobs to perfection.

What followed was another three-peat from 1996 to 1998, featuring a new cast: Dennis Rodman's wild energy, Steve Kerr's clutch shooting, and the ever-evolving Triangle Offense.
